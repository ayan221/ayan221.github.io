PhD Student (D1)  ·  Dept. of Information and Computer Science
School of Integrated Design Engineering, Keio University
Isogawa Lab

I work on computer vision and machine learning, with a focus on human motion generation and millimeter-wave human pose estimation. My research aims to enable privacy-preserving human sensing and high-quality human motion data generation without relying on costly capture equipment. I am also a visiting researcher at Carnegie Mellon University (since Oct. 2024), working on motion generation for humanoid robotics.
